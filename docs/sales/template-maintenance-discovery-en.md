## Offer Fit
Website Maintenance from Website Expert fits businesses that:
- Have a live website that generates revenue, leads, or appointments
- Currently have no one actively updating, monitoring, or backing up their site
- Have experienced or are concerned about downtime, hacking, or site breakages
- Want peace of mind with a defined response SLA (2-hour response for critical issues)

**Poor fit when:** Site is being rebuilt imminently. Client manages a fully custom enterprise application with an in-house DevOps team. Client has a static HTML site with no CMS and zero update requirements.

## Discovery Flow

### Current Site Management
> "Who looks after your website today — an in-house person, your old agency, or no one?"
> "When was the last time plugins, themes, or CMS core were updated?"
> "Are you confident that backups exist and could be used to restore the site quickly?"

### Risk and Reliability
> "Has your site ever gone down unexpectedly? How long did it take to fix?"
> "Have you ever been hacked or had malware on the site?"
> "Do you get any monitoring alerts if the site goes offline?"

### Business Impact
> "How much revenue or business do you lose if your site is down for 1 hour? For 1 day?"
> "Do customers book, buy, or contact you through the website?"
> "Are you running paid ad campaigns that send traffic to the site? Downtime would be wasted spend."

### Updates and Performance
> "Do you know which version of WordPress (or your CMS) you're running?"
> "Are there plugins that haven't been updated in months — do you know the risks?"
> "Have you noticed the site getting slower or receiving security warnings?"

### Support and Response
> "When something breaks on the site, who do you call? How long does it typically take?"
> "Would a 2-hour response SLA for critical fixes give you meaningful business continuity?"
> "Are there any major updates or campaigns planned where you'd need rapid support?"

## Risks and Dependencies
- **WordPress / CMS version** — severely outdated installs may require remedial work before a clean maintenance baseline can be established
- **No existing backups** — if no backups exist, there is no recovery point; first priority is establishing a backup regime
- **Unknown access credentials** — if client doesn't have hosting or CMS admin access, onboarding is blocked
- **Third-party hosting** — if client's hosting is with a provider that restricts access (e.g., locked reseller accounts), managed hosting add-on may be required to provide full monitoring

---

## Assumptions
- Client has or can obtain admin access to CMS and hosting control panel
- Website is on a standard CMS (WordPress preferred) or a platform Website Expert supports
- A 1-hour onboarding session will be needed to set up monitoring and backup tooling
- Minimum monthly engagement (rolling, with 30-day cancellation notice)
